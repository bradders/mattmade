Why this isn't a portfolio

See, all portfolios these days follow the same format. Same witty one line intro, same smattering of trendy buzz words, same glossy screenshots of work, oh and not forgetting the links to social media accounts, all 12 of them. Fuck that.

If you were hoping to form an opinion on my ability as a developer by skim reading my portfolio and glancing at a few high res, drop shadowed screenshots then you are very much mistaken.

Web design/development is a wide ranging and very broad umbrella term, in it you will find many different job titles and skill sets. I'm an Interface developer, which roughly means that my skills lie in HTML, CSS and Javascript. Even though my job is to write code, I still see myself as somewhat of a writer. And the things I write have to be robust, reusable, semantic, efficient and error free. I approach my work in the same way I would writing this. I sweat the details; the little things. I'd like to tell you I haven't fretted over whether to use "class-name, class_name or className" but it genuinely bothers me. Daily. There's something strangely rewarding about giving a shit at a granular level. You wouldn't have a sentence with erroneous punctuation or grammar, so the same should apply to writing code.

I've completed projects for a nationwide furniture seller who's tv/print adverts you see on an hourly basis, a worldwide coffee shop chain that has more stores than is sensibly necessary, a supermarket chain that is big enough to wage war on Denmark, the single best ski resort in the world. Not to mention countless smaller, less glamorous projects. Projects that don't really stand out on a list but ones that probably hold more weight in terms of experience gained and lessons learned.

That doesn't take into consideration the countless other personal projects I manage to waste fill my time with! Some so small and meaningless they barely ever make it online, others actually end up as useful and, dare I say it, good.

The latest of which, whistlerbuses.com was borne out of loathing the paper bus schedule stuck to the fridge, being that it was both hard to read and ripped beyond comprehension.
